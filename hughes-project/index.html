The Negro Speaks of Rivers

I’ve known rivers:
I’ve known rivers ancient as the world and older than the flow of human blood in human veins.

My soul has grown deep like the rivers.

I bathed in the Euphrates when dawns were young.
I danced in the Nile when I was old
I built my hut near the Congo and it lulled me to sleep.
I looked upon the Nile and raised the pyramids above it.
I heard the singing of the Mississippi when Abe Lincoln
went down to New Orleans, and I’ve seen it’s muddy
bosom turn all golden in the sunset.

I’ve known rivers:
Ancient, dusky rivers.

My soul has grown deep like the rivers.

Angel Wings

⏂ originally published in The Weary Blues (1926)
⏀ discusses blackness and race relations through the angels
⏁ themes of struggle and strength
⏂ analysis: white superiority complex/god-complex, beauty/purity in his wings too, wing-like formatting, jazz-syncopated rhythm
⏀ other analysis (Africanizing Jesus Christ): black artists have commonly used Jesus as a vehicle to record and voice sorrows and suffuring, crucifixtion : lynching
⏁ both have wings
